Complaint Procedure

Welcome to the Federal Tax Ombudsman (FTO) Complaint Procedure Page. Follow the steps to file your complaint efficiently.

How to File a Complaint

If you have an issue with the taxation process or a grievance regarding any tax-related matter, follow these steps to file your complaint:

Step 1: Gather Your Information

Collect all relevant details regarding your case. Ensure you have the necessary documents like tax returns, receipts, and other related information.

Step 2: Fill Out the Complaint Form

Complete the complaint form with accurate information. You can submit it online or print it out for physical submission.

Step 3: Submit the Complaint

You can submit the complaint either online, by post, or in person at the FTO office.

Step 4: Complaint Processing

Your complaint will be processed, and you will be notified regarding any actions taken or additional information required.

Step 5: Resolution

The Federal Tax Ombudsman will make a decision based on your complaint, and you will receive the resolution outcome.

Complaint Review Process

The FTO follows a systematic review process to ensure fairness and transparency in resolving complaints. After submission, your complaint will be reviewed by a team of experts who will investigate the issue thoroughly. If any further information is required, you will be contacted directly. The FTO ensures that all complaints are handled efficiently, and resolutions are provided in a timely manner.

Expected Outcomes of Your Complaint

Once the complaint is processed, you will receive one of the following outcomes:

  • Resolution in your favor, with corrective actions taken against the tax authorities if applicable.
  • Rejection of your complaint, with a detailed explanation for the decision.
  • Additional investigation, if the information provided is not sufficient or requires further scrutiny.

Required Documents for Filing a Complaint

  • Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN)
  • Copy of tax return filed
  • Receipts or proof of payment
  • Any relevant correspondence with the tax authorities
  • Supporting documents for the complaint

Eligibility Criteria

To file a complaint with the Federal Tax Ombudsman, you must meet the following criteria:

  • You must be a registered taxpayer in Pakistan.
  • Your complaint should be related to tax administration or tax-related matters.
  • You should have already contacted the relevant tax department or authority, but you are dissatisfied with the response.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the complaint processing time?

Typically, complaints are processed within 15-30 days. You will be informed of any delays.

Can I withdraw my complaint after submission?

Yes, you can withdraw your complaint at any stage. Contact the FTO office for further assistance.

Is there a fee for filing a complaint?

No, filing a complaint is free of charge.
