History of FTO

The Federal Tax Ombudsman (FTO) of Pakistan is an autonomous institution that provides a forum for taxpayers to address their grievances related to tax matters. Established under the "Federal Tax Ombudsman Ordinance, 2000," the FTO acts as a watchdog to ensure that the tax system remains transparent and accountable.

The FTO plays a vital role in resolving tax-related disputes, ensuring taxpayers' rights are upheld, and making recommendations to improve tax administration. Over the years, FTO has served as an effective tool for public service, advocating for taxpayers' interests and providing quick resolution to complaints.

Mission & Vision

Mission: To facilitate an efficient and accountable tax system that promotes fairness, transparency, and justice.

Vision: To foster an inclusive, equitable, and citizen-centric tax system in Pakistan.

Achievements

  • Reduced the average resolution time for taxpayer complaints.
  • Provided free legal assistance to thousands of taxpayers.
  • Advocated for systemic reforms within the taxation framework.
