About FTO Office

Introduction to FTO Office

The Federal Tax Ombudsman (FTO) of Pakistan is an autonomous institution established under the "Federal Tax Ombudsman Ordinance, 2000." The primary goal of the FTO is to provide a mechanism for taxpayers to address grievances regarding tax-related matters, ensuring that their complaints are resolved efficiently and impartially.

The FTO office plays a key role in improving the tax system, promoting transparency, and ensuring that taxpayers' rights are protected. The FTO handles various complaints, provides legal assistance, and advocates for systemic reforms within the taxation framework.

FTO Office History

  • 2000 - Establishment of the Federal Tax Ombudsman Office
  • 2005 - First Major Taxpayer Complaint Resolution
  • 2010 - FTO Office Introduces Online Complaint Portal
  • 2015 - Expansion of Services to Include Taxpayer Education Programs
  • 2020 - Partnership with Government for Tax Reform Initiatives

Key Legislations

  • The Establishment of the Office of Federal Tax Ombudsman Ordinance, 2000
  • Federal Ombudsmen Institutional Reforms Act, 2013
  • Investigation and Disposal of Complaints Regulations, 2001

Mission and Vision

The mission of the Federal Tax Ombudsman is to ensure justice, accountability, and efficiency in the tax administration of Pakistan. The vision is to create a taxpayer-friendly environment that promotes voluntary compliance and trust in the taxation system.

Through continuous reforms and innovative strategies, the FTO aims to resolve taxpayer grievances and ensure the fairness of tax policies and practices.

Services Offered by FTO

  • Addressing complaints related to tax maladministration.
  • Offering advisory services for taxpayers.
  • Providing legal assistance and guidance on tax disputes.
  • Conducting awareness programs to educate taxpayers.
  • Collaborating with government agencies for tax reforms.
